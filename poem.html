Poems

You cry during movie and hearing a sad song,
And support your friends when someone does them wrong.
Pity and kindness to strangers; understanding what they go through,
Save some up and spare some for yourself too.

Don’t push too hard; at times give in to despair and cry.
Just as you owe yourself to live each day and try.
But when each attempt ends in one more closed door,
Cut yourself some slack and love yourself a little more.

Look for joy, as if it were your holy grail,
Know exactly what you want and look for thrill.
Find people who set you free and fly higher than any bird,
See their actions beyond any careful web of word.

Don’t be blind to things you lack, don’t stumble through internal war,
Know yourself, each small flaw and every scar,
Remember life as it is and consequences for every wrong deed you bore,
And count them up, so for each you can love yourself a little more.

A minute to smile,
No complains to file,
How beautiful life would be?
In Earth heaven would be seen.

No wars going on for self gain,
No selfishness or giving pain,
Extinction of every conservative bar,
And life twinkling like stars.

Love among all people,
Peace among war loving fools,
A clean place to live,
True people with nothing to shrive.

Forgiveness for any mistake,
Chain-free be every neck,
The god within worshiped and pleased,
True humanity would be achived.

Banishment of all crimes,
Bringing up the real shine,
Feeling out shining diamonds and gold,
It would be a heavenly world.

Experience from the old,
Young practice to be bold,
A place where souls aren’t sold,
For me it would be heavenly world.

I drew trees when I was eight.
A straight trunk branched into three branches,
A cloud of leaves, undistinguished.
A wall of blue at the back,
Dotted by flocks of birds.
Praises made up for the lack of beauty;
An eight year old Picasso creating wonders.

I can still see the picture in my head,
Or the frame in the nightstand next to my bed,
I am looking into it right now,
And I will it to transform somehow,
To the way, it was, the way it won’t be again,
Then I see my reflection and will the same.

There once was a sunny and gloomy summer day,
And a ship sailed for it won’t lay at bay.
And maybe you were too good to not take along
But I wonder if leaving to you had felt all wrong.

And all the buds you tended and seed you sow,
Grew into blossoms and covered your gardens in row.
Along grew weed and long and dancing grew the grass
Oh how wild and untended that spring was.

Red and orange was world with leaves in stack,
The weather moved on without keeping track
Cold grew the wind and it was end for all to see,
For I knew a fallen leaf can never grace a tree.

I cannot yet bid you farewell nor come along,
But maybe keep you updated thorough a seasonal song
For I am certain one winter you will come and knock
I shall be ready with our pairs of best fuzzy winter socks.

I want a sense of you in the depth of my existence;
An image in my head, you lost in thoughts leaning into a fence.
Your laugh when I forget two failures and recommend the same book thrice
And you still won’t read it and call it my weird vice.

The way you like shades of purple all over your room,
And reassurance u require when you are drenched in gloom.
You sending screenshots of conversations that broke your heart
And words u needed after to make you believe you are smart.

A little yelp of surprise that sometimes escapes your lips,
And how self conscious you get while I wait for next time it slips.
You telling about your happiest memories with a sigh,
These are the moments I want to remember you by.

Maybe i will forget jumbles of names and place,
And every time I repeat your jokes I will make a mess,
But deep in my soul I will see you as clear as any hue,
And that’s the way I want to know you.

I am from Kathmandu,
the land of valleys.
From the cramped and old homes
painted in a landscape of green.
(Places whose name changes
with shifts in the ruling government)
I am from the soil
where I slip every year during monsoon.
I am from the land of blood and sweat loss
but not the land that absorbed it
(Because it was sent abroad and exchanged for lack of hunger)
From the land of few shining cars
but not a single good road to drive them in.
I am from the playground
set up for Dashain
(with swing which creaked every time you went too high.)
From the dry room at the top of the quiet home
that was comfortingly filled with piles of rice.
(which smelled of hard work when cooked.)
I am from the tall old apple tree
that was chopped down after everyone in the house fell from it
But not before it gave scars to remind me of it.

Sun, moon, stars, bulbs and candles we own,
With light we see mountains and materials in each cell of our bone,
One looks up from the surface plain and looks down from height,
But believe me when I say this is a world without light.
 
Prejudice we use as a stick to lead us through our way,
Injustice here has hurt many people every day,
With ray of hope some live through hell,
But the darkness’s strong arm suffocates hope as well.
 
A few here stands to show that courage is still here,
But the changes courage can bring are among the worst of our fear,
So we act too busy following our conservative historic line,
And we are blind towards true heroism’s shine.
 
Instead of innocence we see colour and race,
Other people’s suffering cannot change our pace,
But how can we be blind? Isn’t our world perfect and right?
So, maybe we live in a world without light.
